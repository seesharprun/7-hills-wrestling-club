# Programs

Everything we do is free for current Richmond Public Schools students. The only requirement is a USA Wrestling card for insurance. Our program runs on four pillars designed to grow wrestling across the city.

## Monthly Rotating Practices

We practice once a month on Thursdays, rotating across four RPS high schools. Each head coach hosts one session per month, keeping the commitment manageable while giving athletes exposure to different coaching styles and training partners.

| Week | Host School |
| --- | --- |
| 1st Thursday | John Marshall High School |
| 2nd Thursday | Huguenot High School |
| 3rd Thursday | Richmond High School for the Arts |
| 4th Thursday | Armstrong High School |

Sessions focus on technique, drilling, and live situations — not conditioning. Every experience level is welcome.

## Guaranteed City Matches

Our shared schedule gives every RPS program reliable competition without adding strain to any single school. The season opens with a city quad at John Marshall, then rotates through weekly tri meets in January so every school hosts and every pairing wrestles twice.

| Date | Host | Visitors |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dec 3, 2026 | John Marshall | Armstrong, Huguenot, Richmond |
| Jan 7, 2027 | Armstrong | Huguenot, Richmond |
| Jan 14, 2027 | Richmond | John Marshall, Armstrong |
| Jan 21, 2027 | Huguenot | John Marshall, Richmond |
| Jan 28, 2027 | John Marshall | Huguenot, Armstrong |

## Shared Wrestling Mats

We are working with the school district to create a simple process for moving mats between schools. Shared mat access means any RPS site can host events, combined practices, or clinics — expanding what every program can offer without increasing anyone's budget.

## City Tournaments

We plan to host two city tournaments each season — one in December and one in January. Responsibilities are split between schools so no single program carries the full load. No RPS school pays to compete in an RPS-run tournament.
